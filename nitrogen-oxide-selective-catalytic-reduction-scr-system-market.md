<p><p>The Nitrogen Oxide Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) system market primarily includes two types: ammonia solution and urea. Ammonia solution involves injecting ammonia directly into the exhaust stream, facilitating the conversion of NOx gases into nitrogen and water. Urea, often supplied as an aqueous solution, is hydrolyzed to produce ammonia before undergoing the SCR process. Both methods are employed in various applications, particularly in the automotive and industrial sectors, to achieve stringent emissions regulations and improve air quality.</p></p>
