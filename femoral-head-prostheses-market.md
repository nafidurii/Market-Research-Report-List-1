<p><p>The femoral head prostheses market is primarily categorized into two types: ceramic and metal prostheses. Ceramic prostheses are favored for their biocompatibility and low wear rates, making them suitable for younger, active patients. They provide a smooth articulation but can be brittle. Metal prostheses, often made from cobalt-chromium or titanium alloys, are known for their strength and durability, making them ideal for older patients or those with higher body weight. Each type offers distinct advantages tailored to patient needs and lifestyle.</p></p>
