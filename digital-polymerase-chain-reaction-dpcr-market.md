<p><p>Digital Polymerase Chain Reaction (dPCR) is an advanced molecular biology technique that enhances the precision of DNA quantification. Unlike conventional PCR, which provides relative quantification, dPCR offers absolute quantification by partitioning a DNA sample into thousands of individual reactions. This allows for a more accurate assessment of nucleic acid copies, making dPCR particularly valuable in applications such as oncology, infectious disease diagnostics, and genetic research.</p><p>The dPCR market is anticipated to exhibit significant growth, with an expected CAGR of 6.2% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand for precise and sensitive quantification methods in genetic analysis, coupled with advancements in technology. Additionally, the rise of personalized medicine, along with the growing prevalence of genetic disorders and infectious diseases, is further fueling market expansion.</p><p>Emerging trends include the integration of dPCR with next-generation sequencing technologies, enabling comprehensive genomic studies. Moreover, the development of portable and cost-effective dPCR devices is making the technology more accessible to research institutions and clinical laboratories, thereby enhancing its adoption.
