<p><strong>The Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Alkaline Alcoholysis</li><li>Acidic Alcoholysis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Polyvinyl Butyral (PVB) is a polymer primarily used in adhesive applications and safety glass interlayers. The market for PVB can be segmented into two main types based on the alcoholysis process: Alkaline Alcoholysis and Acidic Alcoholysis. Alkaline Alcoholysis involves the reaction of polyvinyl alcohol with butyraldehyde in the presence of a base, resulting in higher molecular weight PVB. In contrast, Acidic Alcoholysis employs acids to catalyze the reaction, yielding PVB with different properties, affecting applications in automotive, construction, and packaging industries.</p></p>
