<p><p>Low-iron glass is a type of glass with reduced iron content, enhancing transparency and minimizing greenish tint. The market features two primary types: rolled glass, produced by continuous casting and rolling to achieve desired thickness and texture, and float glass, made by floating molten glass on a bed of molten tin for a smooth finish. Both types are favored for applications requiring high optical clarity, such as architecture, automotive, and solar panel manufacturing, catering to aesthetic and functional requirements.</p></p>
