<p><p>The Waste-to-Energy market comprises two primary technology types: thermal and biochemical. Thermal technologies, such as incineration and gasification, convert waste into energy through high-temperature processes, producing electricity and heat. Biochemical reactions, including anaerobic digestion and fermentation, utilize microorganisms to break down organic waste, generating biogas and biofuels. Both methods contribute to sustainable waste management by reducing landfill use while generating renewable energy, thereby addressing environmental concerns and supporting circular economy objectives.</p></p>
