<p><p>Gas Insulated Substations (GIS) are compact and efficient electrical substations that use insulating gas, typically sulfur hexafluoride (SF6), to encase electrical components. The market for GIS is categorized into High Voltage (HV) and Ultra High Voltage (UHV) segments. HV GIS generally operates at voltages from 72.5 kV to 300 kV, suited for urban areas with space constraints. UHV GIS operates above 300 kV, enabling long-distance power transmission and minimizing energy losses, ideal for extensive grid interconnections.</p></p>
