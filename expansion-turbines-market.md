<p><ul><li>Radial</li><li>Axial</li><li>Radial-axial</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Expansion turbines are classified into three main types based on their design and flow direction: radial, axial, and radial-axial. Radial turbines direct gas radially outward from the center, optimizing efficiency for smaller systems. Axial turbines allow fluid flow along the turbine axis, suitable for high-volume applications. Radial-axial turbines combine features of both, enhancing performance across a range of pressures and flow rates. Each type serves distinct sectors, from power generation to refrigeration, catering to specific operational requirements.</p></p>
