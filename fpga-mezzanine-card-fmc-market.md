<p><p>The FPGA Mezzanine Card (FMC) market is divided into Low Pin Count (LPC) and High Pin Count (HPC) types. LPC FMCs typically provide a lower density of signals and are designed for applications requiring basic functionalities with limited power and cost considerations. In contrast, HPC FMCs offer a higher signal density, allowing for advanced functionalities and faster data transfer rates, making them suitable for more demanding applications, such as high-performance computing, telecommunications, and complex signal processing tasks.</p></p>
