<p><ul><li>Bonded</li><li>Coated</li><li>Superabrasive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Abrasive materials are categorized into three main types: bonded, coated, and superabrasives. Bonded abrasives involve grains adhered to a backing material, forming grinding wheels and cutting tools utilized in various machining processes. Coated abrasives consist of abrasive grains adhered to a flexible substrate, such as sandpaper or discs, used for finishing surfaces. Superabrasives, made from materials like diamond and cubic boron nitride, offer exceptional hardness and are ideal for precision grinding and cutting in advanced applications.</p></p>
